About :-
Ellora Caves is another UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the Aurangabad district of Maharashtra, India. It is renowned for its remarkable rock-cut cave temples that represent a harmonious blend of Hindu, Jain, and Buddhist architecture and art.
Brief History: -
The construction of Ellora Caves began in the 6th century CE, during the rule of the Rashtrakuta dynasty. Initially, the caves were primarily Buddhist. The earliest caves are focused on Buddhism, with impressive viharas (monastic dwellings) and chaityas (prayer halls).
