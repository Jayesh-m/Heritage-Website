About :-
The Ajanta Caves are a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a group of ancient rock-cut Buddhist cave monuments located in the Aurangabad district of Maharashtra, India. These caves date back to the 2nd century BCE to about 480 or 650 CE. They are renowned for their impressive architecture, exquisite paintings, and status as a significant historical and cultural monument.
Brief History: -
The earliest caves at Ajanta were believed to be excavated during the Satavahana dynasty, around the 2nd century BCE to the 1st century CE. These caves were primarily designed as simple monastic dwellings and prayer halls for Buddhist monks.
